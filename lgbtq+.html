LGBTQ+

L stands for lesbian.

Definition: a woman who is sexually attracted to people of their own gender.

Big issue #1: Lack of representation

Only 10.2% of characters on television in the 2019-2020 broadcast tv season identified as either lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender or queer.

G stands for gay.

Definition: a man who is sexually attracted to people of their own gender.

Big issue #2: Homelessness

According to the Empire State Colilition of Youth and Family Services, the LGBTQ+ youth makes up about 40% of the homeless youth population in NYC. This group is 8 times more likely to experience homelessness.

B stands for Bisexual

Definition: a person who is sexually attracted not exclusively to people of one particular gender.

Big Issue #3: Discrimination in Healthcare

According to the National LGBTQ Task Force: nearly one in five transgender individuals said they were refused health care because of their gender identity, and a 2017 survey from the Center for American Progress revealed that 25% of LGBTQ+ people have experienced some form of health care bias.

T stands for Transgender

Definition: a person whose sense of personal identity and gender does not correspond with their birth sex.

Big Issue #4: Violence

Not all states have hate crime laws that protect the LGBTQ+ community. In fact, only 32 states include sexual orientation in their hate crime laws, and only 11 states include gender identity in their hate crime laws.

Q stands for Queer/Questioning

Definition: a sexual or gender identity that does not correspond to established ideas of sexuality and gender, especially heterosexual norms.

Big Issue #5: Issues in Shelters

A study by the Center for American Progress and the Equal Rights Center found that only 30 percent of shelters across four states — Connecticut, Washington, Tennessee and Virginia — would house transgender women with other women and 21 percent of those shelters would refuse shelter entirely.

+ stands for all other identities not mentioned previously

Definition: anything not covered in LGBTQ. Ex.(Intersex, Asexual, Pansexual, etc)

Big Issue #6: Discrimination Within the Workplace/School

In a study of LGBTQ+ lawyers across every state Almost 4 of 10 (38.5%, 1,076) of all respondents reported perceptions or experiences of subtle but unintentional biases. More than 1 in 5 respondents (21.7%, 607) noted the experience of subtle and intentional biases.
